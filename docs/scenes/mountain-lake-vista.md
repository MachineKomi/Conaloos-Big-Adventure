## Educational notes

- **Art history:** two paintings of the same place can both be "right." The child can compare this scene with `mountain-lake-childlike` consciously — same place, two ways of seeing. This is the foundation of art-history thinking.
- **Science:** mountains are slow. They were here long before the picture, and will be here long after.
- **Philosophy:** the horizon as an idea that always moves with you.

## Open hooks for future agents

- The "two paintings of the same place" theme is *the* design hook for this pair of scenes. A future scene could be a triptych: childlike, vista, and a third — perhaps a black-and-white sketch — to push the comparison further.
- If `thing_easel` ever lands, drop one in here so the child can imagine the painter who made the vista.
