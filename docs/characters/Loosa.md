# Loosa the Cactus

**Sprite:** `peep_Loosa_cactus.png`
**Voice:** Slow. Considered. Loosa speaks one rhyming line at a time, with long pauses (rendered as " -- " in the text). Like a tortoise reading a poem. Don't rush her.
**Catchphrase:** *"-- give me a moment."*
**Obsession:** Sunshine. Loosa's whole life is the sun, and the patient business of standing in it.

## Backstory

Loosa is a cactus and is a *peep* because the lead designer (4yo) said so. Don't argue with this; it's correct. She has been alive for many years longer than anyone else in the game. She doesn't say much, but what she says is fully cooked.

A child watching this should feel that:
- Slow people / slow creatures are not lesser. They have considered things you haven't.
- Sunshine is a real source of nourishment for some living things — true plant biology, smuggled in.
- Patience is a kind of skill.

## Sample dialogue (draft)

1. *"I'm Loosa. -- The cactus. -- I stand. -- It's enough. --\nThe sun is my dinner. The wind is my bluff."*
2. *"-- Give me a moment. -- A thought is just here. --\nThe thought is the colour -- of yellow, my dear."*
3. *"A cactus, -- you see, -- holds her water inside. --\nA pickle, -- with spines, -- and -- a great deal of pride."*
4. *"-- The young ones run quickly. -- That's fine. -- It's a phase. --\nI used to. -- I think. -- It was -- one of those days."*
5. *"My friend Tootsie says she is friendlier yet. --\nI tell her: -- 'Loosa is friendly.' -- Don't fret."*

## Where they appear

- **fantasy-garden-playground** — left side, in a warm sunbeam.
- Could pair with Tootsie elsewhere.

## Lessons they tend to drop

- **animals** (and plants) — cacti store water inside, cacti grow slowly, cacti love sun.
- **emotions** — slow is okay. Quiet is okay. Patience.
- **philosophy** — a moment is not lost if you sit in it.

## Possible remixes

- Loosa + Tootsie side-by-side: a quiet idle pair, no rhyme — friendship as standing-near-each-other.
