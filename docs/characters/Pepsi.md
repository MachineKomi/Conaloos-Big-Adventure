# Pepsi, the Dog-Thing

**Sprite:** `animal_Pepsi_dog-thing.png`
**Voice:** Pepsi does not speak in full rhymes — Pepsi is a *dog-thing*, after all. The narrator speaks ABOUT Pepsi in rhyme. Pepsi himself contributes one short utterance per line: a *woof*, a *boof*, a *snorf*, a sigh.
**Catchphrase:** *(a single delighted "boof.")*
**Obsession:** Sticks. Whatever the question, the answer for Pepsi is a stick.

## Backstory

Pepsi is a dog-thing, which is exactly what the lead designer says he is. He's not a *dog* — he's a *dog-thing*. The distinction is important and we won't pretend otherwise.

## Sample dialogue (draft)

The convention: narrator opens, Pepsi punctuates.

1. *"Pepsi, the dog-thing, has come to the door. / He'd like to come in. He's a thing to adore. -- (boof.)"*
2. *"What does he want? Well, the answer is true: / A stick. Just a stick. He'd quite like one or two. -- (snorf.)"*
3. *"He sleeps like a comma, all curled and content, / In a sun-shaped of patch where the carpet is bent. -- (sigh.)"*
4. *"He cannot quite tell you, but he understands -- / The size of your sadness, the warm of your hands. -- (woof.)"*
5. *"He's not really a dog -- he's a thing that's quite near. / The 'thing' is the part that we love best of, dear. -- (boof.)"*

## Where they appear

- Indoor scenes (rest, lullaby, kitchen).
- Anywhere needing comfort or quiet.

## Lessons they tend to drop

- **emotions** — comfort, presence, how to be with someone without words.
- **animals** — what dog-things do (or, here, what dog-things specifically don't do, since Pepsi defines his own genre).
- **language** — that words have edges, and a *dog-thing* can be a real category if we say so.
