# Keefa the Wandering Musician

**Sprite:** `peep_Keefa_M_25.png`
**Voice:** A bit older than Lulumi, a bit younger than the parents. Speaks in lines that almost rhyme with themselves — half-echo, half-song. He's the kind of grown-up who treats children like fellow musicians, not like cargo.
**Catchphrase:** *"...did you hear that?"*
**Obsession:** Sound. Every sound. The seagulls, the boats, the way water sounds different at sunset.

## Backstory

Keefa is twenty-five and travels light. He plays a little, sings a little, listens a lot. He shows up at the seaside village around sunset, sits on a low wall, and ends up improvising a song with whichever child wanders by. He doesn't perform — he *makes*, and you're invited.

A child watching this should feel that:
- Music is something you can MAKE, not just listen to.
- Sounds are everywhere — they're already a song you just have to notice.
- Grown-ups can be *fun* without trying too hard.

## Sample dialogue (draft)

1. *"I'm Keefa, I travel, I sing what I find --\nThe sea, and the gulls, and the soft of the wind."*
2. *"Did you hear that just now? It was somebody's bell.\nA bell is a song that decided to dwell."*
3. *"There's no music more old than the music of waves --\nIt's been on the same chorus since back in the caves."*
4. *"A song doesn't ask if it's good or it's right.\nIt just goes, and it carries, and stays through the night."*
5. *"You're humming! Don't stop! That's a song, that one there.\nThe trick is to follow it, just up the air."*

## Lessons they tend to drop

- **language** — sound and song as a kind of language; music as listening before making.
- **science** — how sound travels (gently); why waves sound the way they do.
- **culture-history** — old songs, the idea that music has always been with people.
- **emotions** — humming as a way of feeling, song as comfort.
