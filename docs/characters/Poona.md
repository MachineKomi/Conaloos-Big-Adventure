# Poona, Amelia's Friend

**Sprite:** `peep_Poona_F_4.png`
**Voice:** Same age as Amelia (4) and so the cadence is similar — but Poona's lines turn slightly differently. Where Amelia *names*, Poona *plans*. Where Amelia tells you what something IS, Poona tells you what it WILL BE.
**Catchphrase:** *"...and then we will."*
**Obsession:** Plans. Small ones, made up on the spot, abandoned five minutes later for new ones.

## Backstory

Poona is four, like Amelia, and they are friends in the way only 4-year-olds can be — fully, immediately, and *forever* (this week). She lives in the playground. The two of them invent games faster than the games can be played.

A child watching this should feel that:
- Friendship at four is a kind of bursting energy.
- Plans are wonderful even (especially?) when they don't happen.
- Two friends together are louder than two friends apart.

## Sample dialogue (draft)

1. *"I'm Poona. And me and Amelia are *friends* --\nThe kind of the friends with no proper of ends."*
2. *"And then we will swing! And then we will run!\nAnd then we will tell every flower a pun!"*
3. *"My favourite of plans is to plan a new plan --\nThe planning is fun, even more than the *ran*."*
4. *"A friend is the person who wants what you want --\n(But also has *better* of wants. Just a smount.)"*
5. *"I'm four, and I'm planning a whole afternoon --\nWe'll start with a song, and we'll end with a moon."*

## Where they appear

## Lessons they tend to drop

- **language** — invented words to fit the meter (*smount* — a small amount).
- **emotions** — exuberant friendship as a real, valid mood.
- **philosophy** (gentle) — planning vs. doing, the value of a plan you don't carry out.

## Possible remixes

- Poona + Amelia holding hands in playground: a quiet idle frame.
