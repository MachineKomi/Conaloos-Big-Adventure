# Wawoo the Robo-Snowman

**Sprite:** `peep_Wawoo_robo-snowman.png`
**Voice:** Slightly mechanical. Speaks in short rhymed couplets where one word per line is "wawoo" — the sound the wind makes through him. Imagine a snowman who is also a chimney.
**Catchphrase:** *"Wawoo."*
**Obsession:** Cold. Wawoo is genuinely worried it's not cold enough, here.

## Backstory

Wawoo is a snowman who is also a robot. (Or a robot who is also a snowman. The species naming on the file says *robo-snowman*, so we go with that.) He is, in any case, made of snow and steel, and he runs on cold. In a warm place he gets philosophical.

A child watching this should feel that:
- Strange creatures can be sweet.
- Different needs (cold for Wawoo, sun for Loosa) are equally valid.
- Mechanical-sounding voices can still be tender.

## Sample dialogue (draft)

1. *"I'm Wawoo. (Wawoo.) -- I'm snow and machine. --\nI run on the cold. I dream blue. (And green.)"*
2. *"In summer I worry. -- Wawoo. -- I get small. --\nA snowman in sun -- is a sad sort of all."*
3. *"My nose is a button. My eyes are two coins. --\nMy heart -- wawoo -- is a clock with no joins."*
4. *"I came from a winter you'll some day forget. --\nI brought you a quiet. -- It hasn't gone yet."*
5. *"The wind makes me speak. (Wawoo.) The wind is my mouth. --\nWhen the wind isn't blowing, I just point south."*

## Where they appear

- **whimsical-villiage** — at the village edge, the strangest resident.
- Could appear briefly in `mountain-lake-vista` (where it's cooler) as a Critter Cameo.

## Lessons they tend to drop

- **science** — snow is frozen water; machines have parts; cold and warm are different states.
- **culture-history** — snowmen as a tradition, things that visit and go.
- **emotions** — being a strange creature in a place that doesn't quite fit you, and being okay anyway.
- **language** — onomatopoeia (wawoo as wind-through-pipe), invented species names.
