# Lulumi the Older One

**Sprite:** `peep_Lulumi_F_14.png`
**Voice:** Older — but only by a bit. Patient with the small ones. Speaks in slightly longer rhymes than the children, with more proper nouns and a fondness for "perhaps." She's a teenager, but a kind one.
**Catchphrase:** *"-- perhaps."*
**Obsession:** Lists. Lulumi keeps a list of everything she has noticed today. The list is long.

## Backstory

Lulumi is fourteen. She's not a parent — she's the next-up. She might be a babysitter, an older cousin, the friend-of-the-family who shows up and *gets* the kids. She doesn't talk DOWN to them; she talks slightly UP, and they grow into it.

A child watching this should feel that:
- Older kids can be kind, not scary.
- Growing up doesn't mean losing wonder; it means cataloguing it.
- Lists are a kind of love.

## Sample dialogue (draft)

1. *"I'm Lulumi. I'm fourteen, and that's old, perhaps,\nBut not old enough that I no longer maps."*
2. *"I keep a small notebook of things I have seen --\nA bee with a hat, and a cloud with a sheen."*
3. *"To wonder is how I am most often spent.\nThe questions are mine, and they're paid for by tent."*
4. *"You want to know things? Well, I'll tell you a few:\nThe dew has a memory. The moon has a view."*
5. *"When I was your size I could fit in a coat.\nNow I am bigger -- but coats can still float."*

## Where they appear

## Lessons they tend to drop

- **language** — list-making, cataloguing, the joy of specific words.
- **art-history** — *noticing*, the basis of art. (She's the natural complement to mommy in this respect.)
- **culture-history** — what older kids do for younger ones, the role of the slightly-older.
- **philosophy** — the older you get, the more questions you have, not fewer.
