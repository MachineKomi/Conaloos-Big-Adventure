# Monaloo, the Butterfly

**Sprite:** `animal_Monaloo_butterfly.png`
**Voice:** Quick, light, finishes other people's sentences (especially Conaloo's). Speaks in half-couplets — Monaloo only ever delivers two lines at a time, and the second is always shorter than the first.
**Catchphrase:** *"-- I think so, anyway."*
**Obsession:** Conaloo. Monaloo is convinced that if you take Conaloo's butterly half and let it walk around alone, you get Monaloo. Conaloo gently disagrees, but only gently.

## Backstory

A small, ordinary butterfly with an extraordinary opinion of her relationship to Conaloo. She insists they are *practically the same person*. Conaloo (a bear-butterly) is half-bear, half-butterfly. Monaloo (a butterfly) is, well, a butterfly. The math doesn't quite work, but the affection does.

A child watching this should feel that:
- Two creatures can be very close without being the same.
- Inventing your own family is a kind thing to do.
- It's okay to disagree gently.

## Sample dialogue (draft)

1. *"Hello! I'm Monaloo. -- I'm Conaloo's half! /\nWell, no -- but I might be. (I get a small laugh.)"*
2. *"I land on his shoulder when nobody looks. /\nWe share, when we want to, the same kinds of books."*
3. *"A butterfly tastes with the soles of her feet. /\nWhich means every flower is something to eat."*
4. *"I'm small, and I'm light, and my wings are quite thin. /\nBut my heart is the size of the place I begin."*
5. *"Some friends are decided on. Some are inferred. /\nI inferred mine. (He has not yet demurred.)"*

## Where they appear

- **sunny-rocket-garden** — orbiting Conaloo, occasionally landing on him.
- **mountain-lake-childlike** — by the water, with Conaloo (a Critter Cameo).
- Could appear briefly in any outdoor scene.

## Lessons they tend to drop

- **animals** — butterflies tasting with their feet, how wings work, what makes a butterfly different from a moth.
- **language** — names as choices, similar names that aren't the same name.
- **emotions** — quiet attachment, deciding-to-belong as an act of love.

## Possible remixes

- **Monaloo on Conaloo's head**: rare-response — *"I'm a hat, just for now. -- I'll be off in a tick. / Conaloo's a fine hat-stand. (Such a comfortable stick.)"*
