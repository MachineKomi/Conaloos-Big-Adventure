# Conaloo, the Bear-Butterly

**Sprite:** `animal_Conaloo_bear-butterly.png`
**Voice:** Soft, considered, only slightly fuzzy around the edges. Conaloo is a *bear-butterly* — that's not a typo, that's a species. Half bear, half butterfly, and every word lands like it's been thought about.
**Catchphrase:** *"...mm-hmm, I think so."*
**Obsession:** Wandering. Conaloo is the protagonist; his obsession is the world itself. He has favourite places (the lake), favourite hours (just before evening), favourite weathers (a slow rain), and he tells you these without telling you.

## Backstory

The titular hero. The whole game is *Conaloo's Big Adventure* — and "big" in the way a 4-year-old uses *big*: meaning **enough**, meaning **the whole world**, meaning **the trip from one room to the next room**. Conaloo doesn't go on a quest; Conaloo goes for a walk. The walk is the adventure.

He's a bear AND a butterfly. The bear part is the warm and the slow; the butterfly part is the curious and the brave. He's not torn between them — he's both, and they get along.

## Sample dialogue (draft)

1. *"I'm Conaloo. (That's my name, did you know?) / I'm a bear AND a butterly. (Mostly. Or so.)"*
2. *"My wings are quite small for a bear of my size. / But also, my pawses are big for my eyes."*
3. *"I'm off, just off, for a wander, today. / The going's the thing -- not the where, or the way."*
4. *"A bear-butterly thinks of two thoughts at a time: / The slow ones, in honey. The fast ones, in rhyme."*
5. *"Some days I am bigger. Some days I am small. / Today I'm a Conaloo. (Mostly. That's all.)"*

## Lessons they tend to drop

- **philosophy** — being two things at once, "enough" as a feeling, the trip vs the destination.
- **emotions** — gentle naming of moods, modelling slow consideration.
- **language** — invented words (*butterly*, *pawses*) that fit the meter and the joke, not random ones.
- **science** — how a butterfly's wings work, how a bear hibernates, occasionally.
