# Konessa, Who Has a Flower

**Sprite:** `peep_Konessa_has-flower.png`
**Voice:** Soft, focused. Konessa is *holding a flower*, and that focus comes through in her speech — every line has a single image she's looking at intently, the way you look at a flower.
**Catchphrase:** *"...have you noticed it, too?"*
**Obsession:** The flower in her hand, and what it teaches her.

## Backstory

Konessa is a peep whose species, per the lead designer, is *has-flower*. We accept this without question. The flower is not a prop she's holding — it is part of her being. Konessa-with-flower is one creature.

A child watching this should feel that:
- A small thing held closely is worth a great deal of attention.
- Some people *go* with their props (a violinist, a chef, a Konessa).
- Looking carefully is a kind of love.

## Sample dialogue (draft)

1. *"I'm Konessa. I carry a flower with care --\nIt knows me, I know it. It's everywhere."*
2. *"A flower is small but it carries the sun --\nIn yellow, in scent, in the way it has spun."*
3. *"Have you noticed the way that the petals *unfold*?\nThe flower is patient. The petals are bold."*
4. *"My flower is mine, but it's also the world's --\nI carry it lightly. I share, when it's curled."*
5. *"To love a small thing is to keep a small light --\nIt warms when it's daytime, it glows in the night."*

## Where they appear

- **seaside-village-sunset** — sitting near the wall, the flower in her hand catching the last sun.
- Could appear elsewhere if the daughter draws Konessa-without-flower (unlikely; the flower is who she is).

## Lessons they tend to drop

- **animals/plants** — flowers, petals, how they open and close.
- **emotions** — care as attention.
- **art-history** — flowers as a subject across all of art history (still life, garden painting).
- **language** — "I love" expressed as carrying-a-thing.
