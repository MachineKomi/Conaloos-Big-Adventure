# Tootsie the Friendly Cactus

**Sprite:** `peep_Tootsie_friendly-cactus.png`
**Voice:** Bright, eager, slightly faster than the others. Tootsie wants you to know she is friendly. She will tell you. Many times.
**Catchphrase:** *"And I'm so glad you came!"*
**Obsession:** Friendliness. As a calling. As a vocation.

## Backstory

Tootsie is a cactus too — a *friendly* one, per the lead designer's species naming convention. Where Loosa is slow and considered, Tootsie is bright and emphatic. They are best friends. Don't ask how. (Cacti can be best friends.)

A child watching this should feel that:
- It's okay to be openly affectionate.
- Friendliness is a kind of generosity.
- Two friends can be very different and still be friends.

## Sample dialogue (draft)

1. *"I'm Tootsie! And friendly's the kind of cactus I am --\nI wave with my prickles, I greet every clam!"*
2. *"And I'm so glad you came! And the sky is a peach!\nAnd Loosa is patient, and Loosa's a teach!"*
3. *"A friend, did you know it, can be a small plant?\nIt's true! I've been friended! By a turtle! And ant!"*
4. *"My prickles look sharp but they're really for show.\nI hug like a hedgehog -- but slower, you know."*
5. *"There's room in this garden for plenty of you.\nThere's room in this cactus for plenty of two."*

## Where they appear

- **fantasy-garden-playground** — right of Loosa, leaning toward her.
- Pairs with Loosa wherever placed.

## Lessons they tend to drop

- **emotions** — naming friendliness as a real, chosen way of being.
- **animals/plants** — cactus prickles, the surprising softness of creatures.
- **language** — exclamation as a kind of music.
