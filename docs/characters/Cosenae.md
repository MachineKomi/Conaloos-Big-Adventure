# Cosenae the Older Brother

**Sprite:** `peep_Cosenae_M_5.png`
**Voice:** A confident five-year-old who thinks he knows things. Pronounces words slightly wrong with great certainty.
**Catchphrase:** *"Actually, I think you'll find..."* (which he then mangles delightfully).
**Obsession:** Facts. Or things he believes are facts. Half are real; half are wonderfully made up.

## Backstory

Cosenae is one year older than Amelia and that one year, in his telling, is a whole library. He's earnest, generous, and only sometimes correct.

## Sample dialogue (draft)

1. *"A bee, did you know it, can carry the sea? / I read it in maybe a book, possibly."*
2. *"The ocean has fishes that whisper at noon, / And one of those fishes once wrote me a tune."*
3. *"I'm five. That's a number you can't even hold. / It's bigger than four, it's not quite as old."*
4. *"My sister's named Amelia. (She's smaller than me.) / But she's got the loudest of laughs in the tree."*
5. *"A grown-up once told me the wind has a heart. / I haven't found it yet, but I made a good start."*

## Where they appear

- Hub scenes; outdoor scenes; anywhere a "fact" might be smuggled.

## Lessons they tend to drop

- **science** — half-true confident facts that the parent can quietly correct or extend.
- **language** — playful etymology, mispronunciations as comedy.
- **animals** — bees, fishes, birds, all observed with confident inaccuracy.

## Possible remixes

- Cosenae wearing the rocketship as a hat: "An astronaut hat is a thing I require. / It's good for the *scientific* and good for the *fire*."
